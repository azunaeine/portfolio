Upowallet

Inflation has sharply increased over the years. Covid, the Ukrainian war and the shortage of goods has put pressure on consumers because the prices of goods have become unaffordable. Upowallet aims to help people budget and find unique ways to save money by ensuring that their financial goals are met.

Prototype

Problem

Inflation has caused a lot of financial stress and has impacted the way people spend money. In this world, there are not many options and resources to help educate people to find creative ways to save money. In recent news, there were reports that in the country of Canada, the inflation rate has sharply increased to 8.1%. This is a troubling problem for many Canadians as it shows that the world has sharply changed economically. Presently, there are very few budgeting apps and resources that help people become more financially responsible.

Challenge

How might I develop a web application that educates people on finding creative, unique and efficient ways to save money?

Solution

In response to this problem, I developed UpoWallet. UpoWallet is a budgeting application that educates people and helps them to find creative, unique and efficient ways to save money. Upowallet objective is to help individuals reduce their daily spending limit and save money.

Process

User Research

To get a better understanding of people’s spending patterns and the ways they save money, I conducted 2 user interviews. These interviews were primarily focused on students and working adults as they were the two groups that were primarily concerned with how much they spent money and budgeted it. When interviewing this two groups, we wanted to assess three questions:

      How much do you spend daily or weekly?
      How much money do you save per day or per week?
      Do you use budgeting apps or any resources to help you save money?

Insights

The participants in the interview gave me mixed responses. Most of the students in the interview claimed their tight budget and the high-cost of attending post-secondary institutions was the reason they rarely spent their money. They claimed that they preferred to spend money on things that were absolutely necessary like food. The students in the interview also claimed that they rarely used budgeting apps as they felt it wasn’t necessary based on their low cost of living. On the other hand, working adults spent more money because of their bills and expenses. In addition, working adults claimed they relied on budgeting websites and mobile applications to help them find creative ways to spend money. Design

Brainstorming

The insights that I collected from the interviews helped us improve our features in our application. Furthermore, these insights I collected from the interviews helped us identify weak spots in our application. I created a diagram comparing the insights from the interviews to the features we wanted to use to address the user’s needs.

Wireframe

Me and my teammate started creating low-fidelity wireframes to help us have a visual idea of what we wanted the mobile application to look like. In addition, we created low-fidelity wireframes to have an idea of the features we wanted to prioritize in our application. The low fidelity wireframe was divided into three sections which are the onboarding, the profile settings, and the profile screen.

Visual System

We completed our wireframe and were satisfied with our mobile application's look and structure. From there, we began to think about the visual system. Our primary aim was to use a colour palette that was simple and not too strong.

High Fidelity Design

The high-fidelity design helped me and my partner have a good idea of what our mobile application would like with the presence of colours. I also wanted to test for usability so I wanted my high-fidelity design to be completely polished and look good.
